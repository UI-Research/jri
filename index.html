Justice Reinvestment Initiative (JRI) State Data Tracker

J

ustice reinvestment is a data-driven approach to criminal justice reform designed to examine and address correctional cost and population drivers to generate cost savings that can be reinvested in high-performing public safety strategies. The JRI state data tracker provides information on system-level indicators including prison, probation, and parole populations and overall state savings and reinvestment. While these indicators track trends on key JRI objectives, accurate interpretation requires careful consideration of contextual factors; changes in these measures are impacted by a wide range of policies and practices that extend beyond the reforms passed during JRI engagement.

Georgia

In 2012, Georgia passed comprehensive justice reinvestment reform through H.B.1176 which reclassified certain drug and property offenses, revised sentencing enhancements and mandatory minimums, expanded the use of electronic monitoring, authorized the use of graduated sanctions in response to probation violations, required the use of evidence based supervision practices, and streamlined the transfer process from jail to prison.
